Project Overview

This three-page Power BI dashboard analyzes Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA) data to provide insights into lending patterns, borrower risk profiles, and denial drivers across Massachusetts counties. The analysis supports fair lending compliance monitoring and identifies systemic factors influencing loan approval decisions.

The dashboard enables stakeholders to explore lending trends by purpose, understand demographic impacts on approval rates, and drill down into specific denial reasons using interactive filtering and AI-powered insights.

Dashboard Pages

Page 1: Massachusetts Lending Overview

High-level view of lending volume, loan types (Conventional, FHA, VA, RHS), geographic distribution by county, and trends by loan purpose including home purchase, refinancing, and cash-out refinancing.

Page 2: Borrower Risks & Approval Analysis

Deep dive into approval drivers using Power BI's Key Influencers visual. Analyzes how DTI ratio, LTV, income, and loan purpose impact approval likelihood. Includes demographic breakdowns by age group and income band.

Page 3: Risk and Denial Insights

Explores denial patterns using a decomposition tree to trace paths from loan purpose through income, race, risk flags, to specific denial reasons. Identifies that debt-to-income ratio drives 43% of all denials.

Key Insights

What the Data Revealed

  • DTI is the top denial driver: Debt-to-income ratio accounts for 43% of all loan denials, followed by credit history (23%) and collateral issues (17%)
  • Home improvement loans face highest denial rates: 28.6% denial rate vs. only 5.9% for home purchases
  • Income threshold effect: Borrowers earning $100K+ show near-100% approval rates, while those under $50K face 17% denial rates
  • 2021 refinancing spike: Refinancing volume peaked at 200K+ applications in 2021, then dropped sharply as rates rose
  • LTV sweet spot: Loan-to-value ratios between 79.9-80% correlate with 1.13x higher approval likelihood
